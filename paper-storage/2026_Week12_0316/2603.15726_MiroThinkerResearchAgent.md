## Abstract

We present MiroThinker-1.7, a new research agent designed for complex long-horizon reasoning tasks. Building on this foundation, we further introduce MiroThinker-H1, which extends the agent with heavy-duty reasoning capabilities for more reliable multi-step problem solving. In particular, MiroThinker-1.7 improves the reliability of each interaction step through an agentic mid-training stage that emphasizes structured planning, contextual reasoning, and tool interaction. This enables more effective multi-step interaction and sustained reasoning across complex tasks. MiroThinker-H1 further incorporates verification directly into the reasoning process at both local and global levels. Intermediate reasoning decisions can be evaluated and refined during inference, while the overall reasoning trajectory is audited to ensure that final answers are supported by coherent chains of evidence. Across benchmarks covering open-web research, scientific reasoning, and financial analysis, MiroThinker-H1 achieves state-of-the-art performance on deep research tasks while maintaining strong results on specialized domains. We also release MiroThinker-1.7 and MiroThinker-1.7-mini as open-source models, providing competitive research-agent capabilities with significantly improved efficiency.

## 1. Introduction

Recent advances in LLMs have motivated the emergence of agentic AI systems that interact with tools, environments, and external knowledge sources to solve problems through multi-step reasoning. However, simply scaling the length of reasoning trajectories does not reliably improve performance—when intermediate steps are inaccurate, longer trajectories accumulate noise and propagate errors.

The paper argues that improving long-horizon reasoning requires **scaling effective interaction** rather than simply increasing interaction length. Two key factors are identified:
1. **Strong atomic agentic capabilities** at each step (planning, reasoning, tool interaction).
2. **Verifiable mechanisms** to verify and refine reasoning trajectories during problem solving.

MiroThinker-1.7 is introduced to strengthen step-level reasoning capabilities, while MiroThinker-H1 adds a verification-centric reasoning mode for heavy-duty tasks.

**Key results** (MiroThinker-H1):
- BrowseComp: **88.2** (vs. GPT-5.4 at 82.7, Gemini-3.1-Pro at 85.9)
- BrowseComp-ZH: **84.4** (vs. Seed-2.0-Pro at 82.4)
- GAIA: **88.5** (vs. GPT-5 at 76.4, +12.1 pp)
- FrontierSci-Olympiad: **79.0** (vs. GPT-5.2 at 77.1)

## 2. Related Works

**Agentic LLMs**: Models like GPT-5.4, Claude-4.6, Gemini-3.1-Pro, DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3.5-397B represent a paradigm shift from passive generators to general-purpose autonomous agents.

**Deep Research Agents**: OpenAI Deep Research, Claude Research, Kimi-Researcher, and Grok DeepSearch deploy agentic capabilities at scale. Academic works such as WebThinker, Tongyi DeepResearch, and DeepResearcher explore open-source alternatives. **Agentic mid-training** has emerged as a common strategy (Tongyi DeepResearch, REDSearcher, Step-DeepResearch).

## 3. Agentic Workflow

### 3.1. Formulation

MiroThinker-1.7 builds on the ReAct paradigm, extended with context management and tool-call correction. The agent operates in a **dual-loop** structure:

**Step Loop**: At step \\(t\\), the trajectory log is:

\\[ \mathcal{H}_t^{(e)} = \{(T_1, A_1, O_1), \ldots, (T_{t-1}, A_{t-1}, O_{t-1})\} \tag{1} \\]

A sliding-window index selects the \\(K\\) most recent steps:

\\[ S_t(K) = \{i \in \{1, \ldots, t-1\} \mid i \geq t - K\} \tag{2} \\]

The context operator applies truncation within the window and masking outside:

\\[ \Phi_t(O_i) = \begin{cases} \text{Trunc}_L(O_i), & i \in S_t(K) \\ \emptyset, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \tag{3} \\]

The effective context retains the complete thought-and-action trace:

\\[ \mathcal{C}_t^{(e)} = \{(T_i, A_i, \Phi_t(O_i))\}_{i=1}^{t-1} \tag{4} \\]

Reasoning and action selection:

\\[ T_t = f_\theta(q, \mathcal{C}_t^{(e)}), \quad A_t = \pi_\theta(\mathcal{C}_t^{(e)}, T_t) \tag{5} \\]

**Episode Loop**: If an episode exhausts \\(T_{max}\\) without a valid answer, the agent restarts from the original query:

\\[ \mathcal{C}_0^{(e)} = \{q\}, \quad e > 1 \tag{6} \\] *(no equation 6 in original, but follows naturally)*

### 3.2. Tools

Tools are organized into three categories:
- **Information Retrieval**: `google_search` (ranked results with titles, URLs, snippets) + `scrape_and_extract_info` (content extraction via Jina backend with LLM-mediated summarization).
- **Code Execution**: E2B Linux sandbox (`create_sandbox`, `run_command`, `run_python_code`).
- **File and Data Transfer**: `upload_file_from_local_to_sandbox`, `download_file_from_sandbox_to_local`, `download_file_from_internet_to_sandbox`.

### 3.3. Implementation Details

- **Sliding-window size**: \\(K = 5\\) (five most recent observations).
- **Result truncation**: hard ceiling \\(L\\) on individual observation size; `[Result truncated]` marker appended.
- **Episode restart policy**: clean-slate restart avoids bias from degraded context.
- **Tool call robustness**: framework-level interception and correction of malformed tool invocations.
- **Benchmark contamination prevention**: HuggingFace dataset pages and known leakage sources blocked at infrastructure level.

## 4. High-Quality QA Construction

Two complementary pipelines jointly provide breadth and depth:

### 4.1. Corpus-based Pipeline

Document corpora constructed from highly interlinked sources (Wikipedia, OpenAlex), preserving hyperlink topology. For each seed document, a connected subgraph is sampled via internal hyperlinks; cross-document factual statements are extracted; a strong LLM synthesizes multi-hop QA pairs. Achieves high throughput and broad coverage; however, difficulty control remains implicit.

### 4.2. Web-Augmented Multi-hop Pipeline (WebHop)

Three mechanisms for difficulty control:
1. **Structured Multi-hop Graphs**: directed reasoning trees rooted at the answer entity; each edge represents a verifiable semantic relationship; tree depth controls reasoning hops.
2. **Web-based Semantic Expansion**: root entities from knowledge bases; child nodes expanded via live web search; encyclopedic sources excluded to introduce novel knowledge.
3. **Hierarchical Solvability Verification**: per-level verification that knowing children suffices to narrow parent candidates; root entity must be uniquely identifiable from first-hop neighbors; failed samples rejected before expensive downstream steps.
4. **Adaptive Leaf Obfuscation**: leaf entities likely to leak answers replaced with functional descriptions (e.g., "Louvre Pyramid" → "a royal residence in southern England"); verified against LLM entity inference.

### 4.3. Difficulty-Adaptive Filtering

Post-hoc filtering using agents of varying capability: questions solvable by weaker agents allocated to SFT stages; those resisting stronger agents reserved for RL stages.

## 5. Training Pipeline

Based on open-source Qwen3 MoE models. Four-stage pipeline:

### 5.1. Agentic Mid-training

Strengthens atomic agentic capabilities via a large corpus of agentic supervision spanning:
- **Cold-start planning**: taxonomy-aware planner–judge filtering pipeline.
- **Agentic reasoning and summarization sculpting**: rewriting single turns from multi-turn trajectories; supervision applied only to the rewritten turn.

Training objective (next-token prediction on target assistant turn):

\\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{mid}}(\theta) = -\mathbb{E}_{(\mathcal{C}_{<k}, y_k) \sim \mathcal{D}_{\text{mid}}} [\log \pi_\theta(y_k \mid \mathcal{C}_{<k})] \tag{7} \\]

*(Note: equation numbering reconstructed from PDF content)*

### 5.2. Agentic Supervised Fine-tuning

SFT on expert trajectories \\(\mathcal{D}_{\text{SFT}} = \{(x_i, \mathcal{H}_i)\}_{i=1}^N\\). Each trajectory is a sequence of thought–action–observation triplets. Comprehensive rule-based filtering and data-cleaning applied. Training objective:

\\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{SFT}}(\theta) = -\mathbb{E}_{(x, \mathcal{H})} \left[\sum_{t=1}^{T_{\mathcal{H}}} \log \pi_\theta(T_t, A_t \mid x, \mathcal{H}_{<t})\right] \tag{8} \\]

### 5.3. Agentic Preference Optimization

DPO with preference data from the SFT model. Pairwise dataset \\(\mathcal{D}_{\text{PO}} = \{(x_i, \mathcal{H}_i^+, \mathcal{H}_i^-)\}\\). Preferences determined **solely by correctness** (no structural constraints). Combined objective:

\\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{PO}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{(x, \mathcal{H}^+, \mathcal{H}^-)}[\mathcal{L}_{\text{DPO}}(x, \mathcal{H}^+, \mathcal{H}^-)] + \lambda \mathcal{L}_{\text{SFT}}^{(+)}(\theta) \tag{9} \\]

For MiroThinker-1.7-mini, **preference distillation** transfers alignment signals from the stronger 1.7 model.

### 5.4. Agentic Reinforcement Learning

GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization) in fully online mode. Infrastructure: distributed parallel execution with multi-source web retrieval and LLM-based answer verification.

**Streaming rollout with priority scheduling**: long-tailed rollouts promoted to complete early, preventing their exclusion from distorting training distribution.

**Entropy control**: auxiliary KL penalty on low-probability tokens within negative rollouts prevents premature entropy collapse.

Reward function: \\(R(x, \mathcal{H}) = \alpha_c R_{\text{correct}}(\mathcal{H}) - \alpha_f R_{\text{format}}(\mathcal{H})\\)

GRPO objective with dynamic entropy control:

\\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{GRPO}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}} \mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{H} \sim \pi_\theta} \left[\hat{A}(x, \mathcal{H}) \log \pi_\theta(\mathcal{H} \mid x) - \sum_{t=1}^{|\mathcal{H}|} \beta_{\text{KL}}(t, \mathcal{H}) D_{\text{KL}}(\pi_\theta(\cdot \mid s_t) \| \pi_{\text{ref}}(\cdot \mid s_t))\right] \tag{10} \\]

where \\(\beta_{\text{KL}}(t, \mathcal{H}) = \beta_0 + \beta_{\text{ent}} \mathbf{I}(\hat{A} < 0 \wedge \log \pi_\theta(a_t \mid s_t) < \tau)\\).

## 6. Heavy-duty Reasoning Mode

MiroThinker-H1 extends MiroThinker-1.7 with a verification-centric reasoning scheme at two levels:

**Local Verifier**: prompts the agent to explore more thoroughly and gather selective feedback from the environment at each step, countering habitual thinking patterns. On BrowseComp hard subset (295 questions), local verification alone:
- Reduces interaction steps from 1185.2 to 210.8 (~1/6), a **natural byproduct** of more effective reasoning per step.
- Improves Pass@1 from 32.1 to 58.5 (+26.4 pp), more pronounced on hard questions than the full benchmark.

**Global Verifier**: organizes the full chain of evidence; if insufficient, prompts the agent to resample or complete reasoning rather than delivering a premature answer. Leverages the generation-verification asymmetry: verification is easier than generation.

**Token scaling**: BrowseComp accuracy scales log-linearly. At 16× compute budget: 85.9; at 64× compute: **88.2**.

### 7.5. Effective Interaction Scaling

Comparing MiroThinker-1.5 and 1.7-mini under identical 30B parameter budgets across five agentic benchmarks: 1.7-mini consistently achieves **16.7% better performance** with **43.0% fewer interaction rounds** on average. On HLE specifically: 17.4% better performance with 61.6% fewer rounds. This demonstrates that quality of each step matters more than trajectory length.

## 8. Conclusions

MiroThinker-1.7 and MiroThinker-H1 address the challenges of long-horizon reasoning in agentic AI by emphasizing effective interaction scaling over mere trajectory lengthening. The enhanced training pipeline improves planning, reasoning, and tool use. The verification-centric reasoning mode (Local + Global Verifier) ensures intermediate steps are continuously audited before committing to a final solution. MiroThinker-H1 establishes new state-of-the-art results across BrowseComp, FrontierScience-Olympiad, and FinSearchComp.

Open-source models (MiroThinker-1.7 and 1.7-mini) are available at https://github.com/MiroMindAI/MiroThinker and https://huggingface.co/miromind-ai/MiroThinker-1.7.
